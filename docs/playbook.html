Overview
How this system works

This system scans the full NYSE + NASDAQ universe every Friday evening, scores every ticker across four signal dimensions, and delivers a ranked list with an AI-synthesized thesis per pick. The core philosophy: there are winners every single week in every market condition. The regime tells you where they are, not whether they exist.

Your job is not to predict the market. It's to find the specific tickers that will decouple from the broad tape that week — driven by catalysts, rotation, or structural setups — and enter them at the right time with the right exit plan.
Section 1
Weekly schedule
Fr
Friday evening — open the weekly report
The pipeline runs automatically at 6pm ET. Open your home screen icon, tap Weekly Report. Review the full ranked table. Filter to very_high and high conviction. Read the theses. Build your watchlist for the week — aim for 5-10 candidates. Note the regime banner. It sets the context for everything below.
Su
Sunday evening — catalyst refresh
The system automatically refreshes catalyst signals at 8pm ET Sunday. News breaks all weekend — FDA decisions, acquisitions, analyst notes. The report updates automatically. Check back briefly to see if any tickers moved significantly in rank since Friday.
Mo
Monday 6am ET — final catalyst refresh + pre-market monitor opens
One more catalyst refresh before market open. At 7am ET the pre-market monitor begins checking gap prices every 30 minutes until 9am. Open it at 8:30am CT (your local time). Read the Action column and decision guide carefully. Do not enter trades before reviewing the monitor.
Tu
Tuesday — default entry window
Unless Monday morning signals are clearly favorable (see Entry Procedure below), Tuesday is your default entry day. Weekend noise has settled. Monday's move gives you confirmation. Enter at or shortly after Tuesday open. Log the trade in the Trade Log immediately — ticker, entry price, stop configuration.
Fr
Friday — exit and reset
Exit remaining open positions at or before Friday close (unless a trailing stop already triggered during the week). Log your exit in the Trade Log — exit price, high water mark, exit phase, exit reason. New report drops at 6pm. Repeat.
Section 2
Reading the weekly report

The report ranks every ticker in the universe. Here is what each column means and how to use it.

1
Regime banner (top of page)
Read this first, every time. The regime determines where winners are hiding this week, not whether they exist.
RegimeWhat it meansWhere to look
RISK ON Broad uptrend, risk assets leading High RS, high beta, breakouts
TRENDING / MIXED Uptrend intact, mixed breadth Catalyst + momentum combos
CHOPPY / NEUTRAL No clear trend Specific catalysts, quality names
RISK OFF — MILD Defensive rotation underway Defensives, idiosyncratic catalysts
RISK OFF — SEVERE Market stress Squeezes, FDA, M&A, contra-trend
2
Composite Rank vs Alpha Rank vs EV Rank
Composite Rank — the default sort. Blends alpha score (direction confidence) with EV score (expected value accounting for magnitude). This is your primary ranking.

Alpha Score / Alpha Rank — model confidence that this ticker will be a weekly winner. Currently compressed at the top due to bootstrap labels — use as a filter, not a differentiator.

EV Score / EV Rank — expected value combining win probability and historical magnitude. A ticker with 70% win probability and +8% avg win beats one with 90% probability and +1.5% avg win. Sort by EV Score to surface high-magnitude setups.

Avg Win / Weekly Vol — the ticker's typical weekly upside % and total volatility. Useful for sizing — a ±12% weekly vol name needs a wider stop than a ±3% name.
3
Conviction tier
Based on percentile rank in the universe.
very_high top 7%   high top 15%   elevated top 30%   moderate top 50%

Start your watchlist from very_high and high. Only go lower if those tiers are thin or the thesis is especially compelling.
4
Thesis — click any row to expand
Tickers with a ● blue dot got the full LLM synthesis pass — specific, signal-referenced thesis. Others got rule-based theses. A good thesis names the specific catalyst or signal combination. A generic one ("strong momentum in current market") means the model is less confident about why. Prioritize tickers with specific theses.
Section 3
Entry decision procedure

Follow these steps in order, every time. No exceptions, no vibes.

1
Check regime → set entry day bias
risk_on → bias toward Monday entry
All other regimes → bias toward Tuesday entry

This is a bias, not a rule. The pre-market monitor can override it.
2
Open pre-market monitor at 8:30am CT — read Action column first
The Action column is binary. Act on it mechanically:
SKIP — do not enter. Full stop. No exceptions regardless of how good the thesis looks.
REVIEW — check for negative news. Specific bad catalyst (miss, rejection, downgrade) = skip. No news found = treat as CAUTION.
WAIT — do not enter Monday. Re-evaluate Tuesday morning at open.
CAUTION — proceed to step 3.
WATCH — proceed to step 3.
GO — proceed to step 3.
3
Read Trend + Gap together
Apply these rules mechanically:
MONDAY OPEN — Gap <10% any trend · Gap <25% ramping (↑↑)
MONDAY OPEN — Gap <10% stable (→)
TUESDAY — Gap 10-25% stable · Gap any + fading (↓) over 10%
ENTER SMALLER — Gap 10-25% ramping — reduce size, thesis intact
SKIP — Gap >25% fading (↓↓) — move exhausted, revisit next week
WATCH ONLY — Gap >25% ramping — possible squeeze, do not chase. Wait for first 5-min candle, enter on pullback only. Hard stop at pre-market low.
4
Confirm with sparkline
The sparkline shows each 30-minute check: e.g. +3.1% +3.8% +4.4% +4.7%

Last 2 checks both higher than prior 2 → ramping confirmed
Last 2 checks both lower than prior 2 → fading confirmed
Alternating up/down with no direction → stable, use gap size rule from step 3
5
Tuesday re-evaluation (if deferred)
Check Monday's closing price vs Friday's close:

Monday closed above Friday → thesis intact, enter Tuesday open
Monday closed >5% below Friday → check for news. Bad news = skip. No news = enter
Monday closed within 5% either direction → enter Tuesday open
Section 4
Exit strategy — two-phase approach

Every trade opens with a hard stop. If it moves enough in your favor, the hard stop is cancelled and replaced with a trailing stop. Set both in your broker at entry. Log both in the Trade Log.

Phase 1 — active from entry
Hard stop loss (~7% from entry)
Protects capital if the thesis is wrong immediately. Set at entry, do not move it lower. If this triggers, the trade never activated Phase 2 — log exit_phase as Phase 1. A high Phase 1 rate means the model is sending you into bad setups — the system will self-correct as real labels accumulate.
Phase 2 — activates at +10% gain
Cancel hard stop → set trailing stop (~3-4% from high)
Once the position reaches +10%, you've confirmed the thesis is working. Cancel the hard stop in your broker. Set a trailing stop of 3-4% from the highest price reached. This locks in gains while letting winners run. Log exit_phase as Phase 2 and record the high water mark when you close.
The numbers are starting points, not rules. 7% hard stop and 10% activation threshold and 3-4% trail are reasonable defaults for weekly momentum trades. They should evolve based on what the Trade Log tells you over time — if your weekly vol column shows a stock typically moves ±15%, a 7% stop will get triggered by noise. Size accordingly.
Fri
Default exit — Friday close
If neither stop triggers during the week, exit at or before Friday close. A new report drops Friday at 6pm. You want clean positions going into the weekend.
Section 5
Logging trades

Log every trade — paper or live — immediately at entry and exit. The Trade Log is how the system learns your specific decision quality separate from its own scoring quality.

At entry
What to log
Ticker · Entry date · Entry price · Hard stop % and calculated floor price · Phase 2 activation % · Trailing stop % · Alpha rank from the report · Entry signal reason · Regime at entry · Entry day (Monday vs Tuesday) · Notes
At exit
What to log
Exit date · Exit price · High water mark (highest price the stock reached during your hold) · Exit phase (Phase 1 or Phase 2) · Exit reason (stop_loss / trail_stop / friday_close / manual)

The high water mark is critical — it tells the analysis how much of the peak gain your trailing stop captured vs left on the table.
Section 6
Paper trading period
Recommended: paper trade for 4-6 weeks before going live. The model is on bootstrap labels until real return data accumulates. Use this period to validate the system, tune your stop parameters, and build conviction before risking real capital.
Wk 1-2
Bootstrap phase
Model uses heuristic labels. Scores bunched at top. Use conviction tier + thesis to differentiate. Log paper trades. Watch how real outcomes compare to predictions.
Wk 3-5
Performance log building
Monday scoring workflow collects real returns. Signal attribution analysis begins. Self-refinement starts adjusting weights. Scores begin spreading out meaningfully.
Wk 6+
Model retrains on real labels
LightGBM retrained on actual forward returns. Scores now have genuine predictive meaning. Self-refinement has 6 weeks of signal IC data to work with. Trade Log analysis has enough closed trades to show patterns. This is when you consider going live.
